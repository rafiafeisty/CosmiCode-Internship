After this every year innovations were made and this process is still going on, bringing revolution to the technology after every passing year

Details About the Components

Central Processing Unit(CPU)

The CPU, or Central Processing Unit, is the "brain" of a computer, responsible for executing instructions and performing calculations. It's the primary component that processes data, manages other hardware components, and runs software. Essentially, it's the core component that enables a computer to perform any task.

Motherboard

A motherboard is the central circuit board in a computer that connects all other components, acting as a backbone for communication and power distribution. It provides the foundation for the CPU, memory, storage, and other peripherals to work together.

Random Access Memory (RAM)

Random Access Memory (RAM) is the short-term, high-speed memory in a computer that stores data and instructions currently being used by the CPU. It's volatile, meaning data is lost when the computer is turned off. RAM is crucial for fast and efficient processing, as it allows the CPU to quickly access information needed for running applications and programs.

Hard Disk Drive (HDD)

A Hard Disk Drive (HDD) is a data storage device that uses magnetic platters to store and retrieve digital information. It's a non-volatile storage medium, meaning it retains data even when powered off. HDDs are commonly used as the primary or secondary storage in computers, offering large storage capacities at a relatively low cost per gigabyte compared to other storage technologies like SSDs.

Solid State Drive (SSD)

A Solid State Drive (SSD) is a storage device that uses flash memory to store data, unlike traditional Hard Disk Drives (HDDs) which use spinning disks and mechanical read/write heads. SSDs are known for their speed, reliability, and efficiency, offering faster data access, boot times, and overall system responsiveness. They are also more durable, less prone to damage from drops or shocks, and consume less power.

Power Supply Unit (PSU)

A Power Supply Unit (PSU) is a hardware component that converts AC (Alternating Current) electricity from a wall outlet into stable, low-voltage DC (Direct Current) power that computer components need. It acts as the heart of the computer, providing the necessary power to all internal parts, and is crucial for system stability and performance.

Graphics Processing Unit (GPU)

A Graphics Processing Unit (GPU) is a specialized electronic circuit designed to rapidly manipulate and alter memory to accelerate the creation of images in a frame buffer intended for output to a display. GPUs are crucial for handling graphics-intensive tasks such as rendering 3D images, video editing, and gaming. They are also increasingly used in high-performance computing (HPC), deep learning, and other computationally intensive applications due to their parallel processing capabilities.

Optical Drive (CD/DVD)

An optical drive (also known as an optical disc drive or ODD) is a storage device that uses a laser to read and potentially write data to optical discs like CDs, DVDs, and Blu-ray discs. These drives are commonly found in computers and allow users to play music, watch movies, install software from discs, and create backups.

Cooling System (Fans or Liquid Cooling)

Both air cooling (using fans and heatsinks) and liquid cooling (using a liquid coolant to transfer heat to a radiator) are methods for managing the temperature of computer components. Liquid cooling is generally more efficient and quieter, especially for high-performance systems, but it can also be more expensive and complex to install and maintain.

Sound Card

A sound card, also known as an audio card, is a computer component that enables the input and output of audio signals. It acts as an intermediary between the computer's digital system and the analog world of sound, converting digital data into analog signals for speakers and headphones, and vice versa for microphones.
